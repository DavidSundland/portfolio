David Sundland is a web developer based in Washington, DC. His passions include analytic puzzles, exploration, live music, non-motorized transportation, beer, and the Oxford comma. His first foray into web development was a self-taught project providing a comprehensive live music calendar for DC, using WordPress for the front end and Google Sheets as a makeshift CMS and HTML generator. The thrill of computer-based problem solving prompted him to dive headlong into studying the full stack.

He possesses great attention to detail, strong analytic and problem-solving abilities, & the ability to organize and simplify complex information. His skills include HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, Ajax, REST APIs, Python, Node.js, RegEx, Git, GitHub, React, Redux, MongoDB, and TDD with Mocha and Chai.

David is unique in having a career background that includes highly structured work (municipal employment) and unstructured work (self-employment), work that took place in disparate fields - urban planning and dog training. Throughout those jobs, David has worked to increase productivity via available software, whether through building spreadsheets to automate tasks, using Photoshop to create images that reflect staff's recommendations, or simply creating forms with more logical layouts. Finding no resources that adequately provided information about the live music scene in Washington, DC, he created one, teaching himself WordPress, HTML, CSS, and Python in the process. He recently completed a six-month full-stack development course, studying HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, Node, Express, Mongo, and React.
